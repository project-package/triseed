Corn Varieties

Sweet Corn

High sugar content, tender kernels. Ideal for fresh consumption and canning.

Performance: 85-95% Market: Premium
Quality Indicators:
  • Bright yellow/gold color
  • Plump, uniform kernels
  • No visible damage

Flour Corn

Soft, starchy endosperm. Excellent for milling and baking applications.

Performance: 75-90% Market: Standard
Quality Indicators:
  • Chalky white appearance
  • Soft texture
  • Complete kernel integrity

Waxy Corn

Chewy texture, waxy starch. Prized for Asian cuisine and industrial starch.

Performance: 80-92% Market: Specialty
Quality Indicators:
  • Pearlescent appearance
  • Uniform kernel size
  • No cracks or fissures

Quality Classification

High Quality

Superior seeds with excellent characteristics for planting or consumption.

Criteria:
  • ≥ 90% uniformity
  • Vibrant, consistent color
  • No visible defects
  • Optimal size range
Moderate Quality

Suitable for most applications with minor imperfections.

Criteria:
  • 70-89% uniformity
  • Slight color variation
  • Minor surface blemishes
  • Slight size variation
Low Quality

Limited applications; may have significant defects.

Criteria:
  • < 70% uniformity
  • Discoloration present
  • Cracks or damage visible
  • Irregular size/shape
